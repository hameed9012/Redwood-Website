The tank's mood — where on the dread spectrum?

Same tech, same palette (#0a0a0a base, #b91c1c red). The difference is fog density, light color, and how legible the bottles are at rest. Pick the feeling; everything else gets tuned toward it.

▲
PROPOFOL

A · Clinical Aquarium

bright · sterile · legible

A high-end lab specimen tank. Crisp teal-green water, strong caustics, glass that reads clearly even at rest. Red shows up only in labels/accents. Reads "expensive medical company" first, dread second.

▲

B · Flooded Vault (rec.)

dim · cold · ominous-but-readable

Dim near-black water, cold steel-green tint, heavy depth fog, one slow sweep. A red glow bleeds from the logo behind it. Bottles emerge from haze rather than sit in clear water. Balances "the better good of America" gloss with the darker undercurrent.

▲

C · The Abyss

near-black · murky · most sinister

Almost no ambient light. Objects barely surface from black-green murk; red is the only warmth. Maximum dread — but risks hiding the real glass/refraction you're paying for, and can read as "broken/too dark" on bad screens.

My lean is B: it's the only one that hints at the darker undercurrent and still shows off the real glass + caustics the whole 3D stack exists to render. We can always push a notch darker once it's live.
